Assistive tech · Voice cloning · Personal

2daVoz

When my mother was diagnosed with tongue cancer and faced surgery that would change her speech, I built 2daVoz to bank and recreate her voice — so she can keep communicating, in her own voice, through a touch (AAC) device. A fully local, free pipeline: it recovers her voice from phone-call recordings (speaker-separated with pyannote) plus a clean reading corpus, transcribes with faster-whisper, and fine-tunes a Spanish F5-TTS model on her voice. Synthesis runs offline on a 4 GB laptop GPU — her voice never leaves home and there are no paid services. I also profile her voice acoustically (Praat) and score clone fidelity with speaker embeddings. The on-device AAC layer is already designed — AsTeRICS Grid (an offline PWA) paired with a hybrid voice: an F5 phrase bank plus a fine-tuned Piper voice in SherpaTTS for free text — and the pipeline is validated end to end: on a clean wideband recording the clone already scores as the same speaker (ECAPA embeddings), proving fidelity is gated by capture quality, not the model.
